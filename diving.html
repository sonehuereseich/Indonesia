Bunaken

Bunaken (Indonesian: Pulau Bunaken) is an Indonesian island in the Celebes Sea. It is located about 3 km from the mainland of North Sulawesi near Manado. The village of Bunaken is located at the southern tip of the island, where about 900 people live. The National Underwater Park of Bunaken covers 750 km² and includes the islands of Bunaken, Manado Tua, Siladen, Montehage and Nain. Here divers will find one of the best Indonesian diving areas.

Dive Spots:

  • Ron's Point

  • Pangalisan

  • Kelapa Pendek

Labuan Bajo

Labuan Bajo (also: Labuhan Bajo) is a port city in Indonesia. It is located at the western end of the island Flores. The city is the administrative seat of the administrative district (Kabupaten) Westmanggarai (Manggarai Barat) in the province East Nusa Tenggara. The name of the city means in the Indonesian language as much as "port of the Bajo". The Badjo are an ethnic group originally from Sulawesi. Due to its proximity to the National Park, Labuan Bajo is the main starting point for individual tourists who often travel to Komodo Island by fishing boats. Due to the diverse flora and fauna of the national park also in the sea, there is also a noteworthy diving tourism.

Dive Spots:

  • Mawan

  • Sebayar Kecil

  • Mantapoint
